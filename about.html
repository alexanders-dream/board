History and Foudation

The Counsellors and Psychologist Board was established on the 5th of August 2022. This momentous
occasion was made possible under the provisions of Section 3 of the Counsellors and Psychologists Act No. 14 of 2014, which laid the foundation for the creation of this regulatory body. The core mission of the Board is to regulate and streamline these professions, ensuring that they meet the highest standards of professional practice and ethical conduct. 

This achievement holds immense importance as it not only sets benchmarks for excellence but also ensures consumer protection, instills trust in clients, and fosters the growth and development of these professions in Kenya, ultimately benefiting both practitioners and those seeking their services.

As a State Corporation, the Counsellors and Psychologist Board has a multifaceted role. Beyond its
regulatory responsibilities, it has a broader mandate to promote and expedite economic growth and development while driving social and economic transformation within the country. This mandate extends to instilling good corporate governance practices, enhancing operational efficiency, and improving service delivery to the public, all aimed at maximizing the value for money in the services it oversees.

To execute its duties effectively, the Board comprises a distinguished group of individuals, carefully appointed in accordance with the Counsellors and Psychologist Act of 2014. Among these members is the Registrar of the Board, who serves as its Chief Executive Officer and is entrusted with the day-to-day management of the affairs and staff of the Board, overseeing the professions of counseling and psychology.

Functions of the Board

The Board's functions encompass a wide spectrum of responsibilities, including administering examinations to determine the qualifications of individuals seeking
registration, approving courses for registration purposes, registering and licensing professionals in these fields, and setting the requisite fees. It
also plays a pivotal role in planning, coordinating, and overseeing ongoing professional training and development, facilitating internships for trainee
professionals, and collaborating with relevant bodies to nurture the growth and
development of counsellors and psychologists.

Furthermore, the Board takes an active stance in supervising the professional conduct and practice of its members, including the implementation of disciplinary measures when needed. It establishes a system of professional supervision and even acts as an arbitrator in disputes between licensed professionals and their clients when called upon. Additionally, the Board provides valuable advisory services to the Cabinet Secretary, helping to shape policies, programs, and standards governing the
counselling and psychology professions in Kenya.

In line with its comprehensive role, the Board also issues guidelines to govern various aspects
of the practice of counselling and psychology, ensuring that practitioners adhere to the highest ethical standards.

Ultimately, the Counsellors and Psychologist Board represents a pivotal entity dedicated to the well-being of both professionals and the public, safeguarding the quality of care and
services within the counselling and psychology field in Kenya.
